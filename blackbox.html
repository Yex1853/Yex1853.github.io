Black Box Investigation

The Mystery Black Box, built by Mr. Sinusas, is a box filled with structures that we don't know. Our goal was to try and make a guess on what is in the black box. I investigated the box by pouring water into the box and seeing how much water comes out in order to brainstorm possible structures and I eventually made a replica to test if I was right.

Claim

One key structure I thought was in the black box was a small container on a wooden axle. Its function is to move water into the output funnel (The exit) and to store water. This could be in the black box.

Evidence (Part 1 of 3)

During my observations, I tested the box the way I mentioned before, pouring water in and investigating the amount that comes out. Though, I saw a weird phenomenon after we put the data into a spreadsheet. The water that comes out of the black box is sometimes more than what was poured in. My approach to figuring out how the black box works is looking into this phenomenon while brainstorming a design. I designed and built my model by first making a design on paper and on Photoshop, an Adobe program. Then I used these designs as inspiration and any materials that I thought would be necessary to build the model. My models' key structures were an input funnel on the top, a small container inside placed onto a wooden axle, and an output funnel. The input funnel to transport water into the box, the small container to transport the water out and to hold water, the axle to let the container rotate at around 145 degrees of rotation at a pivot, and an output funnel as an exit point. I tested the replica black box the same way as the original, pouring water inside and seeing how much comes out. The results were very interesting, as the data showed some kind of storage in the box. For example, in trial 6 onward, the input of water was 100 ml and the output was the same, but the water stored was 500 ml, which was a lot higher than what we put in. This wasn't exactly how the original black box stored the water however, as the original was able to let this water out. Also, the data had NO variety somehow after trial 6, as every value was the same (Input was 100 ml, output was 100 ml, water stored was 500 ml for all of them).

Steps Summary

  • I tested the original black box
  • I made a draft of a design
  • I made a final design in Photoshop
  • I used the design and made a replica model
  • I tested the replica just like the original
  • Finally, I compared the two results to see if they were similar

Evidence (Part 2 of 3)

Models are physical representations of a design. They are helpful because they can be used for data which has various uses, like showing variation (Used in the Design Process) in the data, but it also shows, when the model is a replica of another that has its structures hidden, like what we did, how close the replica is to the original model. Their strengths are being used to run tests, which gives a lot more evidence than, say, a design. But their limitations are that they are usually simpler than what they represent regarding data and structures because of oversimplification.

Evidence (Part 3 of 3)

With my data, I can say that a structure close to my small container and wooden axle inside the replica is inside the box. This is because my model was able to replicate the "extra stored water" but some extra structures are needed to release it and to make the variety seen in the original when we tested it.
