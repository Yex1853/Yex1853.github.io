Genetics Experiment

We've made it. Our final experiment. In Quarter 4 of New Media and Tech we were introduced to a way of extracting, cloning and measuring DNA that is very common in the college setting. It starts with taking a tube, putting 50 microliters of extraction buffer, scraping a toothpick in our mouth to grab DNA which includes VNTRs, a special kind of DNA that repeats a lot and changes the amount from person to person, and mixing it into the tube. I wrote my initials on the tube with a marker and put it into an incubator (usually a PCR, which stands for Polyamerase Chain Reaction. I'll get into that later). I took out my tube from the PCR and grabbed another tube, placing Primer Mix and PCR mix into the tube and moving the liquid of the first tube to the second. This new tube is put into the PCR but not to incubate the tube, but rather to clone the DNA. PCRs use Polyamerase and frequent heat changes to make millions of copies of a favorable section of DNA. In this experiment, its the VNTR section. After taking out this tube we made gel for Gel Electrophoresis using an agarose mix. We then prepared a circuit to make opposing charges on both sides of the gel, the top having a positive charge and the opposite for the bottom. When placing DNA it would be compelled to go down since it itself has a positive charge, which this charge phenomena may have made the phrase "opposites attract" a thing. The reason we need this to occur is that the gel has tiny holes that make the DNA practically go through the gel. The smaller the DNA, the further down it goes. We also cover the DNA mixture in gel to keep track of it. When it was finished, we looked through a special black box and saw our results. We took a picture, put it into photoshop, and directly measured how many pixels deep our DNA and other's DNA went down using the Ruler tool. We then put our findings into a specialized google sheet, which gives us an equation for a graph and calculates how many bases the DNA has. We put these findings into a shared google sheet.
